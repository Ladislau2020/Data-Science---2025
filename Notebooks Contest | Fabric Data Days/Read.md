# Fabric Data Days Notebooks Contest - Cities of Tomorrow

As the world’s population becomes increasingly urban, cities stand at the crossroads of innovation and environmental challenge.


From smart infrastructure to green spaces, from urban sprawl to livability - the story of our cities is written in data.

This month, we invite you to explore that story in the **Fabric Community Notebook Challenge: The Cities of Tomorrow – Urban Growth & Sustainability.**


## Your mission?


Use **Fabric Data Science Notebooks** to uncover insights about how cities evolve, sustain, and innovate for the future.

## Challenge Overview

**Focus:** Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), Predictive Modeling, and Data Storytelling

This challenge is open to **data professionals and enthusiasts at all levels** - whether you’re exploring notebooks for the first time or refining your data science craft.

## Dataset

**Primary Dataset:** [Sustainable Urban Planning & Landscape Dataset – Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/programmer3/sustainable-urban-planning-and-landscape-dataset)

This dataset provides structured data for exploring sustainability, land use, infrastructure, and city-level environmental indicators - perfect for understanding how cities balance growth and livability.



## Example columns:
Green Area %, Urban Density, Transport Infrastructure Score, Renewable Energy Index, Pollution Index, Livability Index



## Optional Supplementary Sources:
**World Bank Urban Development Indicators**

**UN-Habitat Urban Data Portal**

## Your Objective
Create a **Fabric Notebook** that tells the data story of _“The Cities of Tomorrow.”_

Your notebook should include:

- Data cleaning and preparation
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Insight generation and (optional) predictive modeling
- Markdown commentary to explain your findings and conclusions

Your goal is to demonstrate how data science can help us understand - and imagine - more sustainable, livable cities.

## Rules
- Use Python or R in Fabric Notebooks.
- All work must be contained in a single .ipynb file.
- Cite all data sources clearly.
- No private or paid datasets.
- Include markdown explanations and conclusions.
- Submit it to Notebooks on Fabric Community
